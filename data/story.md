## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**July 21.** DR Congo stands at **2,473 confirmed cases and 999 deaths**. Add Uganda and France and the global count is 2,494 confirmed and **1,001 deaths**. That is the line the outbreak has not crossed before. A thousand confirmed deaths, and DRC is carrying all but two of them, one short of a thousand on its own.

The crude case fatality ratio in DRC is now **40.4 percent**, and globally 40.1. Two weeks ago it sat at 39 and looked like it might hold there. It did not. Roughly four in ten confirmed cases in this outbreak end in a recorded death, and that is before you count whatever the surveillance system has not reached.

One source carries today's number. [Reuters](https://www.cnbcafrica.com/2026/congo-says-nearly-1000-die-of-ebola-confirmed-cases-rise-to-2473), datelined Dakar, reporting DRC government data: 2,473 cases, 999 deaths, 50 new cases logged in Ituri and North Kivu. BNO posted no daily graphic today, so there is no second wire to line up against it the way Xinhua matched the count yesterday. One source is thinner ground. The figure chains cleanly onto yesterday's, so I seated it and flagged the single-source read in the notes.

Sitrep 10 was due July 19. It is now three days past and still has not appeared. Sitrep 09, data as of July 12, remains the last weekly reconciliation, so every bar after July 12 stays hatched. The gap between the last WHO-reconciled figure (2,145 global confirmed as of July 15) and today's running count (2,494) is 349 cases of surveillance nobody has checked yet. Historically that gap closes upward.

Uganda is still the one thing moving the other way. The 42-day countdown WHO AFRO started on July 16 is running, and Uganda holds at 20 confirmed cases and two deaths. The clock resets to zero if a single new imported case turns up, and DRC transmission has not slowed.
