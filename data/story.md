## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 16.** The daily wire moved up again. The [BNO News graphic for 16 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2066961954879525350), sourced to the DRC and Uganda health ministries and WHO, puts DRC at **837 confirmed cases and 196 confirmed deaths**, up 29 cases and 4 deaths in a day. Uganda holds at **19 confirmed and 2 confirmed deaths**. **Global confirmed: 856 cases, 198 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. Over the trailing seven days the count added 239 confirmed cases and 81 confirmed deaths.

The daily increases have slowed from the record stretch around 14 June, but they have not stopped. DRC's case fatality rate sits near 23.4%, where it has held for a week. Confirmed deaths are now one short of 200. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind this row. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put global confirmed at 827. The gap between that reconciled point and the 16 June wire is two days and 29 cases, narrow by the standard of this outbreak, where the gap ran past 250 cases earlier in June.

The framing hardened one month in. The head of Africa CDC said this could become the worst Ebola event on record and that transmission is still outpacing the response. African Union heads of state met on the outbreak, with President Ramaphosa leading as the AU's pandemic-preparedness champion. WFP, the World Bank, and ECDC all moved this week, on logistics, financing, and a modelling note on how far Bundibugyo could spread.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. North Kivu carried a case fatality rate near 60% in the last province breakdown WHO published, more than three times the rate in Ituri, and Ituri still carries the bulk of DRC's cases. Response operations still run against armed conflict, security incidents at health facilities, and community resistance. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 and 16 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
