## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 29.** WHO published its seventh weekly situation report, and it pulled the late-June numbers up. [Weekly Situation Report 07](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 28 June, puts the outbreak at **1,328 confirmed cases and 379 confirmed deaths** across three countries. DRC carries 1,307 cases and 377 deaths. Uganda holds at 20 confirmed, plus one probable case WHO counts on its own line. France stays at its single imported case. This is the first Sitrep to list France in the country table, so the report now reads Congo, Uganda, France.

WHO's 28 June figure sits above the wire count BNO had carried that day (1,295 cases, 362 deaths). So the 28 June row moves up to the reconciled numbers. The [BNO graphic for 29 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2071756937591402953) then prints the same 1,328 and 379, which means the daily wire has caught up to the Sitrep rather than run past it. The 29 June row sits flat at the reconciled figure. Over the trailing seven days the count added 260 confirmed cases and 110 confirmed deaths.

The death rate keeps climbing. DRC's case fatality ratio reached **28.8 percent**, the highest of this outbreak, up from about 26 percent two weeks ago. WHO puts the overall confirmed rate at 28.5 percent. Part of the rise is backlog, earlier cases getting confirmed as deaths now rather than a sudden drop in survival. The structure has not changed. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated.

CDC's travel notices are unchanged, DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts now treat bars through 28 June as Sitrep-stable; the 29 June bar stays provisional on the daily wire. There is still no row for 27 June. BNO posted no graphic that day, and the tracker leaves the gap rather than invent a number.
