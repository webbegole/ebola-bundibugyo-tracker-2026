## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 25.** The death toll crossed 300. The daily [BNO News graphic for 25 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2070290340859228445), sourced to the DR Congo, Uganda, and France health ministries and WHO, puts **global confirmed deaths at 306**, up 13 in a day. DRC carries 304 of them. So one in four confirmed cases has now died. The DRC case fatality rate sits at about 26 percent, and it has held near that line for two weeks while the case count keeps climbing. A steady ratio against a rising base means the deaths rise with it.

DRC reached **1,155 confirmed cases**, up 37 in a day. Uganda held at **20 confirmed cases** and 2 deaths. France stayed at its single imported case, the doctor who returned from DRC, still isolated and stable. **Global confirmed: 1,176 cases, 306 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. Over the trailing seven days the count added 261 confirmed cases and 72 confirmed deaths.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind these rows. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put DRC at 808 confirmed. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. No France travel notice has published. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 through 25 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
