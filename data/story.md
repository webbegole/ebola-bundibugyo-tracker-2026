## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**July 20.** DR Congo stands at **2,423 confirmed cases and 967 deaths**. Add Uganda and France and the global count is 2,444 confirmed and 969 deaths. The crude case fatality ratio in DRC is **39.9 percent**. It is no longer climbing toward 40. It is there.

Two sources carry the same number today. BNO's daily graphic and [Xinhua](https://english.news.cn/africa/20260721/e03d1aa76f604d4fb91d33061c7a5eec/c.html), reporting from Kinshasa, both give 2,423 cases, 967 deaths, 469 recovered. When the wire count and an independent dispatch land on the identical figure, the running total is doing its job. It is still a running total, not a reconciled one, and that distinction matters below.

The Xinhua dispatch adds the piece the case count alone does not show. A new health zone, Adja in Ituri, has started reporting. That takes the outbreak to 47 health zones across five provinces, and 46 of those 47 have active transmission. Five days ago WHO counted 46 zones with 38 active. So the outbreak is not just adding cases inside places it already occupies. It is adding places, and the places it already had are not closing.

Health workers in Ituri now account for 121 infections and 36 deaths. That is two more infections than WHO recorded on July 15 and the same 36 deaths. The response is still losing staff to the thing it is responding to.

Sitrep 10 was due July 19. It has not appeared, and today is the second day past. Sitrep 09, data as of July 12, remains the last weekly reconciliation, so every bar after July 12 stays hatched. The gap between the last reconciled WHO figure (2,145 global confirmed as of July 15) and today's running count (2,444) is 299 cases of surveillance nobody has checked yet. Historically that gap closes upward.

Uganda is the one thing moving the other way. WHO AFRO published [Uganda begins countdown to end of Ebola outbreak](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/uganda/news/uganda-begins-countdown-end-ebola-outbreak), formalising the 42-day clock that started when the last patient was discharged on July 16. Uganda holds at 20 confirmed cases and two deaths. WHO notes the clock resets to day zero if a single new imported case turns up, and DRC transmission has not slowed.
