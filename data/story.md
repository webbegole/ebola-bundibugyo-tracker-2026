## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**May 27.** Uganda [ordered its border with DRC closed](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/health/uganda-closes-its-border-with-congo-where-suspected-cases-of-rare-ebola-type-are-surging) "with immediate effect" for four weeks. Dr Diana Atwine, Director General at Uganda's Ministry of Health, said emergency crossings for Ebola response, humanitarian operations, cargo, and security will be allowed, with anyone entering placed in mandatory 21-day isolation. The measure goes against WHO guidance, which has warned that border closures push movement to informal crossings that aren't monitored. The Uganda-DRC border runs several hundred miles and is crossed daily by families and traders. Uganda's case load stayed at 7 confirmed and 1 death.

The same day, a DRC Ministry of Health situation report (carried by Xinhua) pushed the cumulative DRC count past 1,000 suspected: **1,077 suspected cases, 238 suspected deaths, 121 lab-confirmed cases, 17 confirmed deaths** across 13 health zones in Ituri, Nord-Kivu, and Sud-Kivu. DRC is also tracing more than 3,000 possible contacts. WHO Director-General Tedros called for a [ceasefire in eastern DRC](https://x.com/DrTedros) to allow safe access for responders, writing that "attacks on health facilities make tracking cases and their contacts nearly impossible." Congolese authorities released the [first known survivor](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/health/uganda-closes-its-border-with-congo-where-suspected-cases-of-rare-ebola-type-are-surging) of the outbreak from a treatment center in Rwampara. The Trump administration said it plans to send Americans exposed to Ebola while abroad to a new facility in Kenya rather than to U.S. hospitals.
