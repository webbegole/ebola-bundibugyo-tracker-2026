## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 22.** The count kept climbing. The daily [BNO News graphic for 22 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2069188090065727998), sourced to the DRC and Uganda health ministries and WHO, puts DRC at **1,048 confirmed cases and 267 confirmed deaths**. Uganda holds at **19 confirmed and 2 confirmed deaths**, unchanged for more than two weeks. **Global confirmed: 1,067 cases, 269 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. DRC added 45 confirmed cases and 13 deaths in a day. Over the trailing seven days the count added 240 confirmed cases and 75 confirmed deaths.

The slope is the story. A day past the 1,000 marker the curve has not bent. DRC added 45 confirmed cases after 47 the day before, so the daily pace is holding, not easing. The case-fatality ratio sits near 25.5 percent.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated, and the people who do that work keep getting sick. The constraint named on 19 June still holds: WHO's emergency director said 75 DRC health workers have been infected since 15 May and 17 have died, and every infected clinician is both a case and a subtraction from the people who trace the next one. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind these rows. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put global confirmed at 827. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 through 22 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
