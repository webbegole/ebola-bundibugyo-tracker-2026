## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 21.** DRC crossed 1,000 confirmed cases. The daily [BNO News graphic for 21 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2068791362657350088), sourced to the DRC and Uganda health ministries and WHO, puts DRC at **1,003 confirmed cases and 254 confirmed deaths**. Uganda holds at **19 confirmed and 2 confirmed deaths**. **Global confirmed: 1,022 cases, 256 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. The 1,003 DRC figure is corroborated the same day by the DRC government's communication ministry, which posted "1,003 cumulative confirmed" in its own situation update. Over the trailing seven days the count added 195 confirmed cases and 62 confirmed deaths.

The number is a marker, not a turn. Helen Branswell at STAT put it plainly: the world recognized this outbreak about five weeks ago, the confirmed count has already passed 1,000, and that is almost certainly an undercount. The thing to read in the milestone is the slope. DRC added 47 confirmed cases on the latest day after 23 the day before, so the curve is not flattening. The case-fatality ratio sits near 25 percent.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. The constraint named on 19 June still holds: WHO's emergency director said 75 DRC health workers have been infected since 15 May and 17 have died, and every infected clinician is both a case and a subtraction from the people who trace the next one. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind these rows. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put global confirmed at 827. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 through 21 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
