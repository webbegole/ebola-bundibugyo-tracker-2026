## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**July 5.** The [BNO graphic for 5 July](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2073986670869811284) puts the outbreak at **1,582 confirmed cases and 508 confirmed deaths**. Deaths crossed 500. DRC carries almost all of it, at 1,561 cases and 506 deaths, a case fatality ratio of about **32.4 percent**. That is another new high. Deaths added 14 in a day and cases added 33. Uganda holds at 20 cases and 2 deaths, with no new case since 21 June. France stays at its single imported case, now recovered.

Watch the ratio, not the count. DRC's case fatality ratio has moved from about 29 percent on 28 June to 32.4 percent on 5 July, in one week. Deaths keep growing a touch faster than confirmed cases, and that widening gap is what the daily wire keeps showing. The last reconciled WHO figure is still [DON612](https://www.who.int/emergencies/disease-outbreak-news/item/2026-DON612) from 3 July, at 1,481 cases and 454 deaths as of 1 to 2 July. The 5 July wire count sits above it and will get reconciled at the next WHO publication.

The structure has not changed. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. The WHO-sponsored trial of two antivirals, MBP134 and remdesivir, is running in Ituri, the first specific treatment tried against this virus.

Weekly Sitrep 07 remains the newest reconciled Sitrep, so the hatching boundary stays at 28 June and the 29 June through 5 July bars stay provisional. There is no row for 27 June. BNO posted no graphic that day, and the tracker leaves the gap rather than invent a number.
