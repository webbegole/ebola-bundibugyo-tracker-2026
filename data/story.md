## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**May 30.** WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus [arrived in Bunia](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/who-chief-visits-congo-ebola-epicenter-as-cases-outpace-response), the epicenter of the outbreak in Ituri Province, to meet local authorities, health workers, and affected families. "This is a difficult situation, and we recognize that. But the Democratic Republic of Congo has faced the Ebola virus many times before. We are confident that it can once again bring this outbreak under control," Tedros said after meeting with DRC Prime Minister Judith Suminwa Tuluka on Friday. He pushed back on the border closures imposed by Uganda, Rwanda, and the Trump administration's entry ban on non-US passport holders who had recently visited DRC, Uganda, or South Sudan: "Closing borders, as some countries have done, only discourages transparency."

[WHO Disease Outbreak News 605](https://www.who.int/emergencies/disease-outbreak-news/item/2026-DON605), published 29 May, reconciles the figures: **906 suspected cases and 223 suspected deaths in DRC as of 27 May; 134 confirmed cases including 18 deaths across both countries as of 29 May** (DRC 125 confirmed, Uganda 9). WHO has not yet reconciled DRC MoH's running suspected count, which BNO News's @BNOFeed daily situation reports put at roughly 1,028 cases by 29 May. DRC MoH ramped up lab testing on 29 May, confirming 100 cases in one day and bringing the DRC lab-confirmed total it reports to 225, ahead of WHO's reconciled 125.

Doctors Without Borders [warned on 30 May](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/who-chief-visits-congo-ebola-epicenter-as-cases-outpace-response) that the response is failing to keep pace: "Never before has an Ebola outbreak recorded so many cases so soon after its declaration. Nobody knows the true scale and severity of this outbreak," said Dr. Alan Gonzalez, MSF's deputy director of operations. A Kenyan court [blocked the US plan](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/kenyan-court-blocks-u-s-plan-to-open-ebola-quarantine-center-to-treat-americansebola-outbreak) to open a 50-bed quarantine center in Kenya for Americans exposed to Ebola.

The Bundibugyo virus has no approved treatment or vaccine. Response operations face armed conflict in Ituri (ADF attacks; M23 controls Goma and Bukavu in the affected Kivu provinces, with rebel-reported cases there), community resistance over burial protocols, and at least three attacks on health centers. EU medical aid reached Ituri on 28 May with more shipments arriving over the following eight days; the US has committed more than $112 million to the response.
