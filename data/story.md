## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 28.** A new WHO reconciliation landed, and it pulled the recent history into focus. [Weekly Situation Report 06](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/uganda/publication/ebola-bundibugyo-virus-disease-outbreak-democratic-republic-congo-2), data as of 21 June, puts DRC at **1,048 confirmed cases and 267 deaths**. That is 240 new confirmed cases and 75 new deaths in the week after Sitrep 05. WHO's 21 June figure matches the wire count BNO had carried for 22 June, the same one-day lag every prior Sitrep has shown. So the June 21 row moves up to those numbers and the apparent next-day jump flattens out. Sitrep 06 also dates Uganda's 20th case to 21 June, two days earlier than the daily wire had placed it.

On the daily wire the outbreak kept climbing. The [BNO graphic for 28 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2071402319799837043) puts **global confirmed at 1,295 cases and 362 deaths**. DRC carries 1,274 cases and 360 deaths. Uganda holds at 20 and 2, France at its single imported case. Suspected cases stay at 116. Over the trailing seven days the count added 227 confirmed cases and 93 confirmed deaths.

The death rate is the number to watch. DRC's case fatality ratio reached **about 28 percent**, the highest of this outbreak, after holding near 26 for two weeks. The last seven days added 227 confirmed cases and 93 deaths. That death-to-case ratio runs well above the cumulative average, which is what pulls the headline rate up. Part of it is backlog, deaths from earlier cases getting confirmed now rather than a sudden change in survival.

The machinery around the response is being rebuilt. WHO and Africa CDC [launched a Continental Incident Management Support Team](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/uganda/news/who-africa-cdc-launch-continental-incident-management-support-team-strengthen-coordination-ebola) on 27 June to coordinate across borders, and Uganda hosted a regional preparedness meeting the same day. The US CDC raised its own emergency response to its highest level on 26 June, though it still rates the risk to the United States as low. None of this changes the structure. Bundibugyo has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is still set by how fast cases get found and isolated.

CDC's travel notices are unchanged, DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts now treat bars through 21 June as Sitrep-stable; 22 June onward stay provisional on the daily wire. There is no row for 27 June. BNO posted no graphic that day, and the tracker leaves the gap rather than invent a number.
