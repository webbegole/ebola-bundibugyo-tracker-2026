## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**July 9.** The outbreak stands at **1,813 confirmed cases and 627 confirmed deaths**. DRC carries almost all of it, 1,792 cases and 625 deaths, a case fatality ratio near **35 percent**. The [8 July figure](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2075080105777336802) added 51 cases and 20 deaths, and the [9 July figure](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2075377805882212425) added another 33 cases and 25 deaths. Uganda holds at 20 cases and 2 deaths, no new case since 21 June. France stays at its single imported case, recovered.

Watch the ratio, not the count. DRC's case fatality has climbed from about 29 percent on 28 June to about 35 percent on 9 July, deaths growing faster than confirmed cases for two weeks running. That is the number to hold onto. The structure has not changed. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. The WHO-sponsored trial of two antivirals, MBP134 and remdesivir, is running in Ituri, the first specific treatment tried against this virus.

The scale is now on the record. STAT's Helen Branswell notes this is already the third largest Ebola outbreak ever recorded, and at 1,792 DRC cases it sits past halfway to the second largest, the 2018 to 2020 DRC epidemic at 3,470 cases.

The last reconciled WHO figures remain [DON612](https://www.who.int/emergencies/disease-outbreak-news/item/2026-DON612) from 3 July, at 1,481 cases and 454 deaths, and Weekly Sitrep 07 as of 28 June. The daily wire count sits above both and will get reconciled at the next WHO publication. The charts treat bars through 28 June as Sitrep-stable, so the 29 June through 9 July bars stay provisional. There is no row for 27 June or 10 July. BNO posted no graphic on 27 June, and BNO's daily graphic runs about a day behind, so no 10 July figure had been released at the time of this update.
