## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 11.** The [BNO News daily graphic for 11 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2065196960382853582), sourced to the DRC and Uganda health ministries and WHO, puts DRC at **676 confirmed cases and 136 deaths** and Uganda unchanged at **19 confirmed and 2 deaths**. Suspected cases hold at 116. **Global: 695 confirmed, 116 suspected, 138 confirmed deaths.** DRC added 41 confirmed cases and 9 deaths in a day. Twenty-seven days since the first case.

The weekly reconciliation I was waiting on landed. [WHO Weekly Sitrep 04](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/uganda/publication/ebola-bundibugyo-virus-disease-outbreak-democratic-republic-congo-0), data as of 7 June, is the first weekly update since Sitrep 03 on 31 May. It puts DRC at 550 confirmed and 101 deaths, an 18.4% case fatality rate, across 25 health zones. Uganda holds at 19 confirmed and 2 confirmed deaths, with one probable case and one probable death that WHO counts in a separate column. So as of 7 June both countries together: 569 confirmed, 103 deaths.

And it settled the question DON606 opened. WHO did not revise the 7 June count down. It revised it up. The same-day wire had DRC at 515 on 7 June. WHO put it at 550, which is exactly what the wire reported a day later, on 8 June. So WHO is not trailing the wire and catching up from below. It is running a day ahead of it, because the backlog confirmations get attributed back to the dates the infections actually belong to. I revised the 7 June row up to match the Sitrep. That is two reconciliations in a row that came in above the running count. The overshoot rule this tracker leaned on for weeks is retired for good.

The worry now is in the geography and the gaps. North Kivu carries 25 confirmed cases and 15 deaths, a 60% case fatality rate, more than three times the rate in Ituri. A WHO expert this week [warned of "blind spots"](https://www.aljazeera.com), saying the true spread is still unknown, and Reuters reported the virus reaching a crowded displacement camp in eastern Congo. Sitrep 04 also raises the assessed risk for countries sharing a land border to high. The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. The Sitrep puts contact follow-up at 60% in Ituri, the epicenter, short of the target, with North Kivu at 80% and South Kivu near full coverage. Ituri alone carries 94% of DRC's confirmed cases. Response operations still run against armed conflict, security incidents at health facilities, and community resistance. With Sitrep 04 in hand, the charts now treat bars through 7 June as Sitrep-stable; 8 June onward stays provisional on the daily wire.
