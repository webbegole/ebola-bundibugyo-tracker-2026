## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 19.** The daily [BNO News graphic for 19 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2068135452133998868), sourced to the DRC and Uganda health ministries and WHO, puts DRC at **933 confirmed cases and 245 confirmed deaths**. Uganda holds at **19 confirmed and 2 confirmed deaths**. **Global confirmed: 952 cases, 247 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. The death count rose 13, back to its earlier pace after the one-day jump of 30 on 18 June. Over the trailing seven days the count added 244 confirmed cases and 106 confirmed deaths.

The cost to the response came into focus today. WHO emergency director Marie Roseline Belizaire said 75 health workers in DRC have been infected since the outbreak was declared on 15 May, and 17 of them have died. [She told reporters](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/19/more-than-70-medics-infected-with-ebola-as-drc-outbreak-spreads-fast) the outbreak is evolving fast and that a system already short of medical staff is paying a high price. That is the mechanism to watch. Every infected clinician is both a case and a subtraction from the people who find and isolate the next one. Africa CDC is pressing for more funding for vaccine development.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind these rows. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put global confirmed at 827. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 through 19 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
