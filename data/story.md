## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**July 1.** The death rate crossed 30 percent. The [BNO graphic for 1 July](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2072492192250151376) puts the outbreak at **1,427 confirmed cases and 440 confirmed deaths** across three countries. DRC added 73 cases and 39 deaths in a day, its steepest daily death count so far, to reach 1,406 cases and 438 deaths. Uganda holds at 20 confirmed and 2 deaths. France stays at its single imported case.

DRC's case fatality ratio reached about **31.2 percent** (438 of 1,406), a new outbreak high, up from 29.9 percent a day earlier and about 26 percent two weeks ago. The +39 deaths in a single day is the largest one-day jump of this outbreak. Some of that is backlog, earlier cases getting confirmed as deaths now rather than a fresh collapse in survival. The structure has not changed. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated, and the count of found cases is still climbing.

WHO has not published Sitrep 08 yet. Sitrep 07 landed around 29 June with data as of 28 June, and the weekly cadence puts the next one in early July, so the 1 July figure is a daily running count above the last reconciled snapshot. The gap between the wire and the last Sitrep is now four days and 99 cases. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest, both last reviewed 15 June. The charts treat bars through 28 June as Sitrep-stable; everything after stays provisional on the daily wire. There is still no row for 27 June. BNO posted no graphic that day, and the tracker leaves the gap rather than invent a number.
