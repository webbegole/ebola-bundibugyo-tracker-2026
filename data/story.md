## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 18.** Deaths jumped. The [BNO News graphic for 18 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2067726779498836215), sourced to the DRC and Uganda health ministries and WHO, puts DRC at **896 confirmed cases and 232 confirmed deaths**. The death count rose 30 in a single day, after a rise of 6 the day before. Uganda holds at **19 confirmed and 2 confirmed deaths**. **Global confirmed: 915 cases, 234 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. Over the trailing seven days the count added 220 confirmed cases and 96 confirmed deaths.

The one-day death jump pulled DRC's case fatality rate to about 25.9%, up from the 23% range where it had held for a week. The DRC health ministry delayed its 17 June figures, so BNO published two updates on 18 June, one for each day. Confirmed deaths crossed 200 on 17 June and 230 on 18 June.

The framing around the outbreak hardened further. The head of Africa CDC repeated that this could become the worst Ebola event on record and said transmission is still outpacing the response, with more than 20,000 contacts still untraced. A wire report this week described an attack on a safe-burial team, the kind of security incident that has slowed containment since the start.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind these rows. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put global confirmed at 827. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 through 18 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
