## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 30.** The daily wire moved past the Sitrep again. The [BNO graphic for 30 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2072096785309270148) puts the outbreak at **1,354 confirmed cases and 401 confirmed deaths** across three countries. DRC carries 1,333 cases and 399 deaths, up 26 and 22 in a day. Uganda holds at 20 confirmed and 2 deaths. France stays at its single imported case. Global confirmed deaths crossed 400 for the first time.

The death rate is pressing on 30 percent. DRC's case fatality ratio reached about **29.9 percent** (399 of 1,333), the highest of this outbreak, up from 28.8 percent a day earlier and about 26 percent two weeks ago. Part of the climb is backlog, earlier cases getting confirmed as deaths now rather than a sudden drop in survival. The structure has not changed. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated.

The map widened too. Media on 29 June reported the outbreak reached [Haut-Uele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Ebola_epidemic), a fourth affected DRC province, carried there by a patient who travelled from the Ituri epicentre. Ituri still holds the bulk of the count, 1,054 confirmed cases across 22 health zones, with North Kivu at 98 and South Kivu at 3. WHO's last reconciliation, Weekly Situation Report 07 as of 28 June, counted 35 affected DRC health zones. A new province is the kind of move that stretches contact tracing thinner.

WHO has not published Sitrep 08 yet. Sitrep 07 landed around 29 June, and the weekly cadence puts the next one in early July, so the 30 June figure is a daily running count above the last reconciled snapshot. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. The charts treat bars through 28 June as Sitrep-stable; 29 and 30 June stay provisional on the daily wire. There is still no row for 27 June. BNO posted no graphic that day, and the tracker leaves the gap rather than invent a number.
