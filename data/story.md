## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 24.** The outbreak now has a third country in the count. France's first Ebola case, a doctor who returned from a humanitarian mission in DRC, moved from a flagged footnote into the running total. The daily [BNO News graphic for 24 June](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2069939446095446177), sourced to the DR Congo, Uganda, and France health ministries and WHO, lists France as its own row with **1 confirmed case** and folds it into the global figure. The patient is isolated and stable, and ECDC still rates the risk to the wider European public as low. One imported case is not transmission in Europe. It is a single traveler caught by the system built to catch them.

The African count kept climbing. DRC reached **1,118 confirmed cases and 291 confirmed deaths**, up 24 cases and 14 deaths in a day. Uganda held at **20 confirmed cases** and 2 deaths. **Global confirmed: 1,139 cases, 293 deaths.** Suspected cases hold at 116. Over the trailing seven days the count added 245 confirmed cases and 89 confirmed deaths. DRC's case fatality rate sits near 26 percent.

The structure underneath has not moved. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. There is no new WHO reconciliation behind these rows. The last one is [Weekly Situation Report 05](https://www.afro.who.int/countries/democratic-republic-of-congo/publications), data as of 14 June, which put DRC at 808 confirmed. CDC's travel notices are unchanged, with DRC's affected provinces at Level 3 and a combined DRC and Uganda Level 2 notice covering the rest. No France travel notice has published. The charts treat bars through 14 June as Sitrep-stable; 15 through 24 June stay provisional on the daily wire.
