## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**July 10.** The outbreak stands at **1,851 confirmed cases and 650 confirmed deaths**. DRC carries almost all of it, 1,830 cases and 648 deaths, a case fatality ratio of about **35 percent**. The [10 July figure](https://x.com/BNOFeed/status/2075727475590484388) added 38 cases and 23 deaths. Uganda holds at 20 cases and 2 deaths, no new case since 21 June. France stays at its single imported case, recovered.

WHO published [Weekly Situation Report 08](https://www.afro.who.int/health-topics/ebola-disease/outbreak-drc-26) with data as of 5 July: 1,645 confirmed cases, 523 confirmed deaths, a confirmed case fatality ratio of 31.8 percent, and 12,417 contacts still to follow. That is the first reconciled figure since Sitrep 07 on 28 June, and it revised the 5 July row up by 63 cases and 15 deaths. Bars through 5 July now render solid on the charts. Everything after 5 July stays provisional.

Watch the ratio, not the count. DRC's case fatality has climbed from about 29 percent on 28 June to about 35 percent on 10 July, deaths growing faster than confirmed cases for two weeks running. The structure has not changed. Bundibugyo still has no approved vaccine or treatment, so the ceiling is set by how fast cases get found and isolated. Reuters reports a WHO official saying the outbreak is still spreading largely undetected, and Africa CDC calls it escalating. The PARTNERS trial of two antivirals, MBP134 and remdesivir, has begun enrolling patients in Ituri, the first specific treatment tried against this virus.
