## Story so far

The 2026 Ebola outbreak began in early May in the Democratic Republic of the Congo's Ituri Province. The earliest known symptom onset is April 24; authorities formally declared the outbreak on May 15. Laboratory analysis at the Institut National de Recherche Biomedicale identified the pathogen as **Bundibugyo virus (BDBV)**, one of four orthoebolavirus species that cause Ebola disease in humans. Unlike the more common Zaire ebolavirus, Bundibugyo currently has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic.

The World Health Organization declared the outbreak a [Public Health Emergency of International Concern](https://www.who.int/news/item/17-05-2026-epidemic-of-ebola-disease-in-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-uganda-determined-a-public-health-emergency-of-international-concern) on May 17. Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on May 18. Within the first ten days, cases spread across 12 health zones in three DRC provinces (Ituri, Nord-Kivu, Sud-Kivu) plus imported and locally-transmitted cases in Kampala, Uganda.

The response is operating under acute constraints: armed conflict in the affected zones, community distrust of containment measures (multiple Ebola treatment centers have been attacked, with patients escaping), high population mobility, and the absence of a Bundibugyo-specific vaccine. Healthcare-associated infections in Uganda raise concerns about undetected transmission chains in urban centers.

## Latest update

**June 2.** WHO and CDC reconciled the case count today and pulled it down. WHO spokesperson Christian Lindmeier briefed reporters in Geneva that DRC stands at **321 confirmed cases, 116 suspected, 48 deaths, and 6 recoveries**, per [Reuters via CNBC Africa](https://www.cnbcafrica.com/2026/who-says-suspected-ebola-cases-drop-to-116-after-hundreds-ruled-out). The CDC website carries the same 321 / 116 figures with an explicit note: "On May 29, the DRC Ministry of Health updated their total suspect case count to remove suspected cases that have been ruled out after investigation and suspected deaths that are pending the results of ongoing investigation." Uganda's Ministry of Health then [announced six new confirmed cases](https://english.news.cn/africa/20260602/74e9d674de0f4d7b9215a49167a54f70/c.html) in the afternoon, all identified among contacts of prior confirmed patients, bringing Uganda's total to 15 confirmed and 1 death. Twelve patients are currently admitted; two have been discharged. **Global: 336 confirmed, 116 suspected, 49 confirmed deaths.**

Yesterday's row is revised in place. The wire-source June 1 figure of 343 DRC confirmed (BNO) overshot DRC MoH's formal 31 May situation report of 321 confirmed; the WHO and CDC updates today align the tracker with the underlying official source. DRC confirmed for June 1: 343 → 321.

The DRC MoH 31 May situation report (carried by [Outbreak News Today June 2](https://outbreaknewstoday.substack.com/p/ebola-outbreak-in-the-drc-tops-300)) lists **23 health zones affected** across Ituri, Nord-Kivu, and Sud-Kivu, including a newly impacted health zone (Logo) with 12 new confirmed cases. That's up from the 13 health zones noted earlier in the outbreak. The report also notes 238 patients under treatment, six recoveries on the day, a 43% contact follow-up rate, community resistance in Bunia and Nizi, and ongoing security issues that limit humanitarian access. Africa CDC director-general Jean Kaseya, in [a Financial Times op-ed Sunday](https://www.cnbcafrica.com/2026/who-says-suspected-ebola-cases-drop-to-116-after-hundreds-ruled-out), cited more than 1,100 suspected cases under investigation before the cleanup; today's 116 figure reflects the surveillance system catching up to the laboratory results.

Two suspected Brazilian cases tested negative; an Italian suspected case also cleared. Dr. Abdou Sebushishe of the International Medical Corps in Goma told [CBS News](https://www.cidrap.umn.edu/ebola/who-drastically-downsizes-ebola-case-count-dr-congo-outbreak) that as many as 20% of case-patients are healthcare workers and that the outbreak could take "beyond six months" to control. The Bundibugyo virus still has no approved treatment or vaccine. Response operations face armed conflict in Ituri (ADF attacks; M23 controls Goma and Bukavu in the affected Kivu provinces, with rebel-reported cases there), community resistance over burial protocols, and attacks on health centers. EU medical aid reached Ituri on 28 May with more shipments arriving over the following eight days; the US has committed more than $112 million to the response. A Kenyan court [blocked the US plan](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/kenyan-court-blocks-u-s-plan-to-open-ebola-quarantine-center-to-treat-americansebola-outbreak) to open a 50-bed quarantine center in Kenya. The Government of the DRC and WHO closed their joint Bunia mission on 31 May with a [joint statement](https://www.who.int/news/item/31-05-2026-joint-statement-by-the-government-of-the-democratic-republic-of-the-congo-and-who-concerning-the-outbreak-of-ebola-disease-caused-by-the-bundibugyo-virus) asking other governments to keep "borders remain open" so medical supplies and personnel can move. WHO Sitrep 03 was expected today; the Geneva briefing served as the reconciliation channel in its place.
